About Back Deck

Backyard grilling, brought downtown.

Back Deck offers professional backyard grilling in a laid-back city setting, seven days a week. Friends and family meet here for charcoal-grilled favorites inspired by casual cookouts, alongside porch-perfect cocktails, beer and wine.

Why Charcoal

We burn hardwood charcoal. On purpose.

Hardwood charcoal is made by partially burning oak and maple with limited oxygen, which eliminates the more volatile components — trapped hydrogen, methane and tars — for a more predictable, even burn and far less smoke than raw wood.

It also gives us real advantages over gas. Besides the pleasant smoky flavor, charcoal burns hotter — up to 100° higher than a gas flame — so food sears better, delicate items like fish stick less, and the charred, “grilled” taste that comes from a hot grate and real smoke is much more pronounced.

A skilled griller also has more control over charcoal than gas: a charcoal fire has many levels of heat, so if you can read the fire, you can adjust the temperature under any piece of food just by moving it to a hotter or cooler spot on the grate.
