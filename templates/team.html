Paul Sussman

Chef / Co-Owner

The art of grilling is in Paul’s DNA. His earliest memories of it are asados — the carnivorous feasts traditional in South America — thrown by his mother’s Chilean family during his childhood in Lexington, Massachusetts. Paul graduated from Brandeis University with a degree in psychology, but a lifelong love of food led him to a job cooking at The Fishmonger’s Café in Woods Hole, Cape Cod.

He went on to hone his craft at the acclaimed Providence restaurant Al Forno, where he manned the charcoal grill and wood-fired brick oven for two years. In 1991 he opened Daddy-O’s in Cambridge, blending regional American cuisine with his Jewish and South American heritage; it ran for nine years before he opened Macondo Latin Grill in 2001, focused on flame-cooked South American food. He later served as executive chef at The Fireplace in Brookline and opened the kitchen at Z Square Cambridge in Harvard Square.

Paul remains devoted to “quality, a love of lively flavors and a desire to offer socially and environmentally conscious food at reasonable prices.” He contributes to culinary publications, sits on the board of Operation Frontline Boston, and is a charter member of Chefs Collaborative, a nonprofit promoting local, sustainable food systems.

Bradley Fredericks

Proprietor

Brad grew up in Easton, Massachusetts and graduated from Babson College before leaving for Houston, Texas, to work as a CPA for Exxon — where he discovered a culinary culture that shared his craving for all things grilled. After years of fajitas and barbecue in Houston, he headed further west for an MBA from UCLA in California.

Recognizing that Boston lacked good Tex-Mex and barbecue, Brad opened Fajitas & ’Ritas in Downtown Crossing in 1989, which grew into several Boston locations. He and his wife Maria later launched Finnegans Wake, a Cambridge restaurant serving Irish cuisine.

In 2011, Brad partnered with Chef Paul Sussman to open Back Deck, built around charcoal-fired grilling, on West Street near his original Downtown Crossing location. He remains active in the neighborhood’s growth, serving on the Advisory Board for the Downtown Boston Business Improvement District and the Massachusetts Restaurant Association’s board. An amateur grill-master himself, Brad keeps a year-round entertaining setup in his Jamaica Plain backyard — his own rib-eye recipe inspired Back Deck’s signature Cowboy Steak. He and Maria have four children.
